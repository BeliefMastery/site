Core Methodology

Three Rotating Roles

Client, Counselor, and Witness with specific operational tasks.

Client maintains eye contact and retains full authority. Counselor's single task: keep the client talking without evaluation or advice. Witness embodies group containment, supporting counselor discreetly while maintaining focused presence.
Minimum Conditions

Group size, time discipline, consent protocols, and role separation.

3-5 participants per working unit. Visible timers ensure equal turns. Explicit consent for proximity, touch, and eye contact each round. Role separation prevents counseling someone who is the topic of your processing. Continuous hazard management required.
Session Flow

Structured format from preparation through closing circle.

Check-in with volatility numbers determines order. Client chooses counselor, witnesses huddle close. Timed session with counselor keeping client talking. Attention-out process for regulation. Closing circle reviews technical discoveries and positive exit.
Attention-Out Process

Left-brain activating questions to help regulate after deep processing.

Counselor leads client through math problems, describing objects from memory, or other cognitive tasks to shift out of emotional processing state. Critical for safe transition and preventing psychic fragmentation.

Guiding Values and Rules

Equality Through Time Discipline

Timers ensure equal opportunity to speak and be heard.

Unequal exchanges ("free-clienting") are avoided. Equal turns increase attendee retention and maintain structural integrity. Time discipline is non-negotiable for container reliability.
Privacy & Integrity

What is shared in the group stays in the group.

Permission required to reference another's content. Confidentiality may only be broken at threshold of life and limb. This creates safety for deep vulnerability and honest processing.
Own Your Responsibility

Practice defining boundaries and learning to reject and accept rejection.

Participants must develop capacity to voice boundaries clearly. Learning to both reject and gracefully accept rejection builds sovereignty and prevents dependency patterns.
No Evaluation or Advice

Do not impose interpretations or solutions.

All client answers treated as correct. Counselor's role is curiosity and prompts, not rescue or evaluation. This maintains client's authority over their own experience and prevents imprinting.
Safety as Reliable Containment

Safety defined not as absence of challenge but as reliable structure.

Anchored in consent, clear boundaries, and disciplined practice. Safety is what makes transformation possible—not protection from difficulty but reliable container for navigating it.
