Understanding the Scale (0-10):

  • 0-2 (Not at all / Never): The symptom or experience does not apply to you at all, or occurs extremely rarely.
  • 3-4 (Minimal / Rarely): The symptom occurs occasionally but has minimal impact on your life.
  • 5-6 (Moderate / Sometimes): The symptom is present with moderate frequency or intensity, and has some noticeable impact.
  • 7-8 (Significant / Often): The symptom occurs frequently or with significant intensity, and has a noticeable impact on daily functioning.
  • 9-10 (Extreme / Always): The symptom is nearly constant or extremely intense, and significantly impairs daily functioning.

Guidelines for Accurate Assessment:

  • Answer based on your experiences over the past 2-4 weeks, unless the question specifies otherwise.
  • Be honest with yourself - there are no "wrong" answers.
  • Consider how the symptom affects your daily life, relationships, and functioning.
  • If unsure, choose the middle range (4-6) and proceed - the system will refine based on your responses.
  • You can go back and change answers using the "Previous" button.
