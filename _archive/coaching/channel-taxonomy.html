1. Appendix 4. Channel Taxonomy



Flow analysis for all 42 channels of inter-node dynamics


    1. Introductory note:

      1. This appendix expands the Node and Channel Model from Chapter 9 into a full index of the forty-two active connections between the seven core nodes. Each channel is a conduit - carrying sensation, emotion, thought, or vision between faculties.

      2. The taxonomy functions as a working diagram for observing influence and exchange within the system.

Open channels allow balanced integration.

Blocked channels isolate nodes from stabilizing inputs.

Overloaded channels distort perspective and coherence.

      1. These patterns are dynamic. Tracking them over time reveals where your system integrates easily, where it strains, and where deliberate rerouting will restore flow. Treated as a living framework, the map will adapt alongside your own development.



    1. Flow Originating from the Root

        1. Root informs Sex

        2. (Grounding; Primal Life Force) → (Creativity and Will)

Healthy Flow (Abundance):

When the Root is abundant with physical energy and vitality, the Sex center responds with increased creative output and strong willpower. You feel energized, passionate, and eager to engage in both creative and physical pursuits with enthusiasm.

Healthy Flow (Lack):

When there is a lack of grounding or physical vitality, the Sex center reacts by dampening creative and sexual energy. You might experience a drop in motivation, a lack of inspiration, or reduced desire for creative and passionate activities, signaling a need for physical revitalization.

Blocked Flow:

When the channel between the Root and Sex center is blocked, the response becomes inappropriate or misaligned. You may experience frustration, creative stagnation, or even compulsive behaviors without a clear direction. This reflects a disconnect between physical vitality and the ability to channel it into purposeful creative or willful action.
