Identified Tactics

These are specific manipulation tactics that may be present in your relationship. Each tactic includes examples and explanations in plain language.

Covert Entrench
How it works: Hidden/Subtle - Hard to notice, sneaky manipulation • When it happens: Control Phase - Happens once they have you, to keep you trapped
Example:

"You're so lucky to have me. No one else would put up with you like I do."

What it does:

This tactic subtly reinforces your dependency by making you feel that the manipulator is doing you a favor by being with you. It creates a sense of indebtedness and makes you question your own worth.

How it controls you:

By making you feel lucky to have them, they position themselves as valuable and you as less valuable. This creates dependency because you start to believe you can't do better or that you need them for validation.

Emotional Blackmail
How it works: Hidden/Subtle - Hard to notice, sneaky manipulation • When it happens: Control Phase - Happens once they have you, to keep you trapped
Example:

"If you really loved me, you would do this for me. I guess you don't care about me after all."

What it does:

This tactic uses your emotions against you by making you feel guilty or responsible for their feelings. It manipulates your desire to be a good partner or person.

How it controls you:

By making you feel guilty or like a bad person, they control your behavior. You start doing things you don't want to do just to avoid feeling guilty or to prove you care.
