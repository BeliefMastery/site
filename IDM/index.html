Executive Summary

Site Machinery Ltd, based at 105 Pascoe Street, Nelson, is well-positioned to expand sales of IDMC's DeSite SLG-series topsoil screeners (e.g., SLG-108VFRB, SLG-78VFRB, SLG-68V) and related attachments like telehandler bins. These portable, low-maintenance screeners process 15-45m³/hour of materials such as soil, gravel, and compost, enabling small-to-medium earthmoving businesses to repurpose waste into sellable grades—directly addressing high landfill dumping fees of $266-305 per tonne at York Valley Landfill. With New Zealand's earthmoving equipment market projected to grow through 2031 amid a 1-4% contraction in overall construction in 2025, the strategy shifts from inefficient Facebook ads (current CAC ~$1,750) to targeted, low-cost tactics emphasizing visibility, demos, and ROI storytelling. Goal: Achieve 5-10 sales in Year 1 at <$500 CAC, leveraging local networking and peer influence as seen in Australian trends.

Objectives

  • Sales Targets: Secure 5-10 unit sales in the first 12 months (focusing on Nelson/Tasman region, expanding NZ-wide), generating $250K-$500K revenue (based on $50K average unit price ex-GST/freight).
  • Efficiency Goals: Reduce customer acquisition cost (CAC) to under $500 per sale via organic/referral channels; aim for 20% lead conversion from demos.
  • Brand Awareness: Increase local visibility among 200+ small/medium earthmovers, measured by 50% growth in inquiries via phone/email (current: +64 3 979 7602, info@sitemachinery.co.nz).
  • Long-Term: Build a referral network for 30% of future sales through user testimonials.

Target Market

Focus on small-to-medium businesses (SMBs) in earthmoving, construction, landscaping, and rural sectors within a 200km radius of Nelson (e.g., Tasman, Marlborough). Key segments:

Segment Description Size/Opportunity Pain Points
Urban/Town Contractors (e.g., Nelson builders) SMBs handling site prep for residential/commercial projects. ~150 firms; resilient infrastructure spend offsets 2025 residential dip. High dumping fees ($266/tonne rising to $311 in 2026/27); contaminated soil adds $50K-$60K per project.
Rural Earthmovers/Farmers Lifestyle blocks, roading contractors creating soil/stone grades for drains/roads. ~100 operations; untapped as soil often dumped unscreened. Low-value waste; need for on-site processing to create sellable products (e.g., drainage metal).
Landscapers/Quarries Medium firms repurposing compost/gravel for resale. ~50 sites; growing demand for recycled materials. Labor costs vs. buying in screened product; peers' usage drives desire.

Positioning & Messaging

Position IDMC screeners as "Your On-Site Profit Maker: Screen Waste into Wealth, Skip the Dump Fees." Emphasize:

  • Cost Savings: Recover $200-$500/tonne in fees by separating soil into grades; potential $500/hour earnings from processed materials.
  • Ease & Visibility: Portable (544-907kg), excavator-compatible, no engine/belts—demo-ready for job sites.
  • Peer Proof: "See it in action locally—join the earthmovers turning dump costs into driveway gold."

Tailor messaging: Rural = "Grade Your Gravel On-Farm"; Urban = "Ditch $300/Tonne Fees—Repurpose Today."

Tactics & Channels

Ditch broad Facebook ads; pivot to high-ROI, relationship-driven channels proven for heavy machinery sales (e.g., demos, content, partnerships). Allocate 60% budget to offline visibility, 40% digital.

Channel Tactics Expected Impact Budget Allocation (Year 1: $20K Total)
Demo Days & Visibility Host 4 free on-site demos at local hubs (e.g., construction sites, rural co-ops); wrap 2 company vehicles with screener imagery. Partner with peers for "peer showcase" events where users demo units. Drives word-of-mouth; 1 sale/3 demos per Australian trends. Low cost, high networking. $8K (fuel, signage, giveaways).
Content Marketing Produce 12 short videos (1-2 min) of Nelson demos showing ROI (e.g., "From Dump to Drain Rock in 1 Hour"). Share via YouTube/LinkedIn; email to 500 local contacts quarterly. Case studies from Australian users adapted for NZ fees. Builds trust; SEO for "soil screener Nelson" searches. $4K (video production, email tool like Mailchimp).
Targeted Digital LinkedIn ads to 5K NZ earthmovers (geo: Nelson/Tasman; keywords: "dumping fees," "soil screening"); join local groups. Retarget site visitors with ROI calculator tool on www.sitemachinery.co.nz. $1-2/click; 10% conversion vs. FB's 0.4%. $4K (ads, tool dev).
Partnerships & Networking Collaborate with 5 local suppliers (e.g., fuel depots, quarries) for co-branded flyers; attend low-cost Nelson Chamber events (not trade shows). Referral program: $500 credit per successful intro. Leverages relationships for 30% leads; key for NZ market entry. $2K (materials, incentives).
Direct Outreach Cold-call/email 100 SMBs from directories; follow up with personalized ROI quotes (e.g., "Save $10K/year on 40 tonnes soil"). High conversion (20%) for pain-point focused. $2K (CRM like HubSpot free tier).
