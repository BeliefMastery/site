Initial Marketing Strategy for IDMC Soil Screeners

Site Machinery Ltd, based at 105 Pascoe Street, Nelson, is launching sales of IDMC's DeSite SLG-series topsoil screeners (e.g., SLG-108VFRB, SLG-78VFRB, SLG-68V) and related attachments like telehandler bins. These portable, low-maintenance screeners process 15-45m³/hour of materials such as soil, gravel, and compost, enabling small-to-medium earthmoving businesses to repurpose waste into sellable grades—directly addressing high landfill dumping fees of $266-305 per tonne at York Valley Landfill. With New Zealand's earthmoving equipment market projected to grow through 2031 amid a 1-4% contraction in overall construction in 2025, the strategy focuses on initial low-cost tactics emphasizing visibility, demos, and networking in the small NZ market, drawing from Rob's Australian experience.

Urgent Market Shift: The escalating US-China tariff war, with an additional 100% tariff (up to 130%) on Chinese goods starting November 1, 2025, has already caused a 50% shutdown in Chinese manufacturing this week. Secure equipment now to avoid supply disruptions and price hikes.

High Dumping Fees

$266-305/tonne at York Valley Landfill, rising to $311 in 2026/27—repurpose on-site to skip entirely (urban pitch).

Expanded Detail: In Nelson, urban contractors face $50K+ per project in fees for contaminated soil. IDMC screeners separate fines from aggregates in under an hour, turning waste into reusable topsoil and gravel—recover costs immediately and comply with NZ waste regs without off-site hauling.

Contaminated Soil Handling

Project delays from sorting—quick separation saves time (town contractors).

Expanded Detail: Manual sorting risks non-compliance with Resource Management Act; delays add 20-30% to timelines. Portable SLG units (544-907kg) hook to excavators for vibration-free screening, isolating contaminants fast—ideal for Tasman sites where access is tight.

Unscreened Waste

Dump low-value soil; grade into sellable products (rural landscapers).

Expanded Detail: Rural ops dump 40+ tonnes unscreened yearly, missing $500/tonne resale value for drainage metal. Screen on-farm to create 3 grades (topsoil, 10mm, 20mm) for local sales—boost revenue by 15-25% per job, as seen in Australian rural pilots.

Labor & Sourcing

Manual work or buy-ins—portable screeners cut both.

Expanded Detail: SMBs spend 10-15 hours/week on sorting, at $50/hr labor—buying screened product adds $100/tonne. DeSite's no-engine design processes 15-45m³/hr with one operator, slashing costs by 40% and freeing crew for billable work in Marlborough vineyards or Nelson builds.

Expanded Detail: 50% Chinese factory shutdowns this week mean 3-6 month delays for alternatives. IDMC's US-based stock ensures quick Nelson delivery—lock in pre-tariff pricing to avoid 20-30% hikes, protecting your 2026 ops amid construction slowdowns.
