Marketing Strategy for IDMC Soil Screeners

Site Machinery Ltd, based at 105 Pascoe Street, Nelson, is well-positioned to expand sales of IDMC's DeSite SLG-series topsoil screeners (e.g., SLG-108VFRB, SLG-78VFRB, SLG-68V) and related attachments like telehandler bins. These portable, low-maintenance screeners process 15-45m³/hour of materials such as soil, gravel, and compost, enabling small-to-medium earthmoving businesses to repurpose waste into sellable grades—directly addressing high landfill dumping fees of $266-305 per tonne at York Valley Landfill. With New Zealand's earthmoving equipment market projected to grow through 2031 amid a 1-4% contraction in overall construction in 2025, the strategy shifts from inefficient Facebook ads (current $7 per click, one sale per 250 clicks) to targeted, low-cost tactics emphasizing visibility, demos, and ROI storytelling.

Urgent Market Shift: The escalating US-China tariff war, with an additional 100% tariff (up to 130%) on Chinese goods starting November 1, 2025, has already caused a 50% shutdown in Chinese manufacturing this week. Secure equipment now to avoid supply disruptions and price hikes.

Objectives

  • Lead Generation: Generate 100 qualified leads in the first 12 months via demos and outreach.
  • Efficiency: Shift to organic/referral channels for higher conversion (aim 20% from demos).
  • Brand Awareness: Increase local visibility among 200+ small/medium earthmovers, measured by 50% growth in inquiries via phone/email (current: +64 3 979 7602, info@sitemachinery.co.nz).
  • Long-Term Growth: Build a referral network for 30% of future leads through user testimonials.

Target Market

Focus on small-to-medium businesses (SMBs) in earthmoving, construction, landscaping, and rural sectors within a 200km radius of Nelson (e.g., Tasman, Marlborough). Key segments:

Segment Description Size/Opportunity Pain Points
Urban/Town Contractors (e.g., Nelson builders) SMBs handling site prep for residential/commercial projects. ~150 firms; resilient infrastructure spend offsets 2025 residential dip. High dumping fees ($266/tonne rising to $311 in 2026/27); contaminated soil adds project delays and extra handling.
Rural Earthmovers/Farmers Lifestyle blocks, roading contractors creating soil/stone grades for drains/roads. ~100 operations; untapped as soil often dumped unscreened. Low-value waste disposal; lack of on-site processing limits sellable product creation (e.g., drainage metal).
Landscapers/Quarries Medium firms repurposing compost/gravel for resale. ~50 sites; growing demand for recycled materials. Labor-intensive sorting vs. buying screened product; competitive pressure from peers adopting efficient tools.

Pain Points for Cold Calls & Targeted Advertising

Use these targeted pain points in outreach scripts, ad copy, and personalized emails to resonate with prospects. Frame solutions around IDMC screeners' ease and immediate value.

High Dumping Fees

$266-305/tonne at local landfills—escalating to $311 soon. Repurpose on-site to avoid fees entirely.

Contaminated Soil Handling

Extra $50K+ in project costs from sorting delays. Quick separation into clean grades saves time and compliance headaches.

Unscreened Waste Disposal

Rural dumping wastes potential revenue. Turn low-value soil into sellable drainage/road materials in hours.

Labor & Sourcing Costs

High labor for manual sorting or buying pre-screened product. Portable screeners cut both, boosting margins.

Supply Chain Disruptions

US-China tariffs (100-130% from Nov 1) causing 50% Chinese manufacturing shutdowns—secure equipment now before shortages.

Competitive Edge

Peers using screeners for faster jobs and networking buzz. Don't lag—demo locally to see the difference.

Positioning & Messaging

Position IDMC screeners as "Your On-Site Profit Maker: Screen Waste into Wealth, Skip the Dump Fees." Emphasize:

  • Cost Recovery: Separate soil into grades to repurpose waste, dodging $200-$500/tonne fees.
  • Ease & Visibility: Portable (544-907kg), excavator-compatible, no engine/belts—demo-ready for job sites.
  • Peer Proof: "See it in action locally—join the earthmovers turning dump costs into driveway gold."

Tailor messaging: Rural = "Grade Your Gravel On-Farm"; Urban = "Ditch $300/Tonne Fees—Repurpose Today." Leverage tariff urgency: "Act Before Nov 1: Tariffs Threaten Supply."
